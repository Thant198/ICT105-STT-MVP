# Lab 06 Case Brief

## Case: Campus Life Management System (CampusLife Hub Prototype)

University students often miss important announcements, project deadlines, and campus activities because information is shared across multiple platforms such as LINE, Messenger, Facebook, Google Classroom, and Email. Students also find it difficult to track project tasks, access shared files, and communicate effectively with their team members.

## Target Users

- University students
- Project team members
- Club leaders
- Lecturers / Administrators

## Product Direction

A web-based platform that centralizes campus announcements, project management, deadlines, events, and team collaboration. The system allows students to view announcements, manage project tasks, access shared files, track deadlines, browse campus events, and receive notifications. Administrators can create, edit, approve, and manage system records.

## Lab 06 Focus

Design the business model, technical architecture, data flow, and data structure for the Campus Life Management System. Ensure that all design decisions are supported by the customer discovery evidence, user stories, and MVP features developed in previous labs.

# Case Study Brief: Campus Life Management System

## Problem Context

University students often miss important announcements, project updates, event information, and academic deadlines because information is scattered across different platforms such as LINE groups, email, LMS, and social media. As a result, students struggle to find accurate information quickly and may miss important activities or submissions.

## Practice Purpose

This case is used to validate whether a centralized Campus Life Management System can help students access important campus information more efficiently and reduce missed announcements, deadlines, and project updates.

## Example MVP Experiment Direction

The team wants to test whether students can use a simple web prototype to view announcements, check project information, browse campus events, receive notifications, and complete common tasks without confusion.

## Example Critical Assumption

Students will prefer using one centralized campus information platform if it is faster, easier to use, and more organized than searching across multiple communication channels.

## Example Experiment Type

Simple web prototype with task-based usability testing.
